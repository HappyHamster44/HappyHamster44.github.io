Pbv 302

The Pbv 302 is a Swedish armored infantry fighting vehicle that was first introduced in the early 1960s. It was designed to provide highly mobile and well-protected infantry support on the battlefield, and was used extensively by the Swedish Army for several decades.
The Pbv 302 is a tracked vehicle that features a spacious, fully enclosed compartment for the infantry squad and a turret-mounted 20mm cannon. It has a top speed of around 70 km/h and is powered by a diesel engine. The vehicle is also equipped with smoke grenade launchers and a range of other defensive systems to help protect the crew and infantry from enemy fire.
Despite being an older design, the Pbv 302 remains a capable and effective vehicle in many roles. Its armor provides good protection against small arms fire and shrapnel, and its powerful cannon is capable of taking out a range of enemy targets. The vehicle has also been used in peacekeeping missions and other operations where its mobility and versatility are highly valued.
Overall, the Pbv 302 is a reliable and well-regarded armored vehicle that has served the Swedish Army well for many years. While it has been largely replaced by newer designs in recent years, it remains a respected and effective weapon system that continues to see service around the world.
